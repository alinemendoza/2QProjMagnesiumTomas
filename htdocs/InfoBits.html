Did You Know?

I am sure some of the information I put here are old-but-gold bits you haven't heard of yet. Even I was surprised at collecting and reading these pieces of knowledge! With that, here are 10 interesting feminist facts.

Josefa Llanes Escoda, the woman in our 1,000 peso bill, was the founder of the Girl Scouts of the Philippines. Because of choosing to assist Filipino soldiers, she (unfortunately) got arrested and beheaded by Japanese officials.

Eva Estrada-Kalaw was the senator responsible for notable laws such as instituting DSWD, initiating barrio schools in provincial areas, and increasing salary fees of public school teachers. Being on the opposition side, Marcos prevented her from authoring other bills. How come? ü§î

Encarnacion Alzona was a pioneering supporter of suffrage. She was the first Filipina to hold a Ph.D. and became an active history professor on equal rights. She is known for saying: "A person enjoying full political rights deserves greater respect and esteem than a disenfranchised one." This quote is about her opinion on women gaining the right to vote.

The word feminism originated from its French counterpart, feminisme coined by Charles Fourier, a French philosopher. Back then, this term described the "ideal" feminine qualities. Until now, it does not hold any malicious intent towards the male sex nor aim to juxtapose itself.

Women are paid less despite working the same job as men. In the US, women only earn 54 cents for every man's dollar. That's equivalent to women only getting 27 pesos for every 50 pesos a man has. This alarming situation is due to many factors such as chosen occupation, gender discrimination, part-time work, and so many more. üôÑ

Liliosa Hilao was an astute student known for being the first student activist to die during the Martial Law regime. When drunken soldiers barged into their family's house to arrest her suspected brother, she asked for a search warrant. The soldiers then arrested her and later abused, tortured, and killed her. Afterward, the military said she committed suicide by "drinking muriatic acid."

The word suffrage means "a right to vote." Its usage significantly grew when women fought for their freedom to be citizens like their male counterparts.

Paz Marquez-Benitez was a brilliant literary figure who wrote the first Filipino modern short story, "Dead Stars" in English. She was part of the first Filipino generation to learn under the American schooling system. She soon had a remarkable reputation as an influential storyteller.

Purita Kalaw-Ledesma founded the Art Association of the Philippines. She is regarded by many as a contemporary art innovator and is known as the Mother of Modern Philippine Art. By voicing her support for many art-related issues, she revolutionized the Filipino painting era we have today.

Despite the popular Maria Clara image held dearly by our nation, women during ancient times were strikingly fiercer and had more representation than before. Babaylans then were women who (supposedly) had magical healing and spiritual powers. They were political officials who also acted as priestesses and healers.
